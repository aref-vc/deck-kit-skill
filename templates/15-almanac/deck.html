Abstract

We study the evidence first, then say what it means. This report sets out a decade of continuous field measurement across eighteen sites and asks a single question · how do communities adapt when the records that describe their environment fall out of date faster than they can be revised. Our method is plain. We measure on a fixed schedule, log every reading with full provenance, test the data against stated hypotheses, and publish the methods alongside the result so that any reader may check the work. Research Pillars06

Section IV · Method

Research pillars

  • i.Observe.Measure in the field on a fixed schedule, with calibrated instruments and recorded conditions at every visit.
  • ii.Record.Log every reading with full provenance · site, time, instrument, observer, and the raw value before any correction.
  • iii.Analyse.Test the data against stated hypotheses, declare the method in advance, and report the result whether or not it confirms.
  • iv.Review.Submit methods and results for outside scrutiny before any claim leaves the institute.
  • v.Publish.Release the findings together with the underlying data, so that any reader may reproduce the work.
